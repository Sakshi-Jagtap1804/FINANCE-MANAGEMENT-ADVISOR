Public Provident Fund(PPF)

Public Provident Fund (PPF) is a popular long-term investment scheme in India. It is a government-backed savings and investment plan that offers several benefits to investors.

Key details about PPF

☛ Eligibility: PPF accounts can be opened by Indian citizens, including minors (with a parent or guardian as the account holder), as well as Hindu Undivided Families (HUFs). Non-resident Indians (NRIs) are not eligible to open a new PPF account, but those who already have an account before becoming an NRI can continue to operate it until maturity.

☛ Account Opening: PPF accounts can be opened at designated post offices and authorized bank branches across India. The account can also be transferred from one bank/post office to another if needed.

☛ Tenure: The PPF has a fixed maturity period of 15 years. However, it can be extended in blocks of 5 years after the initial 15-year period.

☛ Minimum and Maximum Contribution: You can open a PPF account with a minimum deposit of Rs. 100 and make an annual contribution of at least Rs. 500. The maximum annual contribution is Rs. 1,50,000.

☛ Interest Rate: The interest rate on PPF is set by the government and is subject to periodic revisions. Historically, PPF offers a higher interest rate compared to regular savings accounts. The interest is compounded annually.

☛ Tax Benefits: PPF investments offer tax benefits in India. The contributions made to the PPF account are eligible for a deduction under Section 80C of the Income Tax Act. The interest earned and the maturity amount are also tax-exempt.

☛ Withdrawals: Partial withdrawals are allowed from the 7th year onwards. The amount that can be withdrawn is subject to certain limits and conditions. Full withdrawal is allowed upon maturity (after 15 years) or upon the death of the account holder.

☛ Loan Facility: From the 3rd year to the 6th year, PPF account holders can avail of a loan against their PPF balance. The loan amount is subject to certain limits, and the interest rate on the loan is typically lower than that of personal loans.

☛ Nomination: Account holders can nominate a family member to receive the proceeds of the PPF account in the event of their demise.

☛ Account Extensions: After the initial 15-year maturity period, account holders have the option to extend their PPF account in blocks of 5 years. They can choose to continue the account without making fresh contributions.

☛ Transferability: PPF accounts can be transferred from one authorized bank or post office to another, and also from one location to another within India.

☛ Limitations for HUFs: While HUFs are eligible to open PPF accounts, they are not allowed to claim tax benefits under Section 80C of the Income Tax Act.

☛ No Attachments: PPF accounts are immune to attachment under any order or decree of a court.

☛ Risk Level : Low to Nil.

✔✔ It's important to note that the rules and regulations regarding PPF may change over time, so it's advisable to check with the latest guidelines and consult a financial advisor for the most up-to-date information and personalized investment advice.
